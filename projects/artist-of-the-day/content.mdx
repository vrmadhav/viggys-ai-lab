## The idea

Most people want exposure to art, not a syllabus. Artist of the Day is a quiet daily
ritual: open the app, meet one artist, read a short essay generated from public sources,
and see three representative works. The goal is to make art history feel like a habit
rather than homework.

## Why it exists

The project started from a simple question: could an art-learning app feel like a
daily pause instead of a course? The shape is intentionally small. One artist, one
short read, a few works to sit with, then you leave with a little more context than
you had before.

## Current shape

The first version is in progress. The core loop is a daily artist page with a concise
essay, representative works, and enough source context to keep the generated writing
grounded.

## Notes

The interesting tension is pacing. If the app explains too much, it becomes homework.
If it explains too little, it becomes a pretty image feed. The sweet spot is a small
ritual that gives someone just enough to notice more the next time they see the work.
