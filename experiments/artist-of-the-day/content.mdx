## The idea

Most people want exposure to art, not a syllabus. Artist of the Day is a quiet daily
ritual: open the app, meet one artist, read a short essay generated from public sources,
and see three representative works. The goal is to make art history feel like a habit
rather than homework.

## The prompt

A few rounds with an LLM turned the vague "an app that teaches art" into a researched
brief: who the audience is, what a single session should feel like, where the source
material comes from, and what "good" looks like for the daily essay. That brief became
the prompt both builds start from.

## Prompt-Only build

The prompt was handed to an AI app builder to see what it would produce on its own —
no further direction. This track is in progress; the build and its decisions will be
linked here.

## Design-Thinking build

Before writing a line of product, this track starts with real user research and design
thinking — what a daily reader actually wants, how the ritual should open and close,
and where AI helps versus gets in the way. AI is used for the coding only.

## The verdict

Did the design work earn its keep? Side-by-side, which build is the one you'd actually
open every morning? The comparison lands once both tracks ship.
